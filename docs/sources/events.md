# Event Sources

References for event dates, categories, civilizations involved, and significance levels.

## Primary Chronologies

- Stearns (2001). Primary source for event dates and civilizations involved across all 105 events.
- Grun (2005). Cross-reference for year-level dating accuracy.

## Ancient and Classical Events

- Bickerman (1980). Date conventions for pre-Common Era events.
- Bauer, Susan Wise. *The History of the Ancient World: From the Earliest Accounts to the Fall of Rome*. New York: W. W. Norton, 2007.
  Bronze Age and Iron Age events: invention of writing, Battle of Kadesh, founding of Rome, Greco-Persian wars.

## Medieval and Early Modern Events

- Wickham, Chris. *The Inheritance of Rome: Illuminating the Dark Ages, 400--1000*. London: Penguin, 2009.
  Fall of Western Roman Empire, Rise of Islam, Viking expansion, Carolingian period.
- Abulafia, David. *The Great Sea: A Human History of the Mediterranean*. Oxford: Oxford University Press, 2011.
  Crusades, Fall of Constantinople, Mediterranean trade events.

## Modern Events

- Hobsbawm, Eric. *The Age of Revolution: 1789--1848*; *The Age of Capital: 1848--1875*; *The Age of Empire: 1875--1914*; *The Age of Extremes: 1914--1991*. London: Weidenfeld and Nicolson, 1962--1994.
  French Revolution through 20th-century events.
